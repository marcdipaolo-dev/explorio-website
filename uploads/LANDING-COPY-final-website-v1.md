## WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

The v1 copy was too literary. Real users aren't dreaming about cities whispering secrets — they're escaping a specific, deeply familiar experience: the bored guide, the upsell, the group of forty, the audio tour that goes silent and stays silent. They want a knowledgeable friend who walks at their pace and actually answers questions.

Two critical data points that reframe everything:

1. **The ability to ask questions is the single biggest unmet need in the category.** Every competitor is one-directional. Not one app lets you say "wait, tell me more about that." This is Explorio's signature move and should be front and center.

2. **Users hate AI-sounding content more than almost anything.** "AI slop," "2-minute Wikipedia read with robo voice," "lacks personality" — this is the most damaging complaint across every competitor that uses AI. The copy must pre-empt this. Never say "AI-powered." Position the guide as knowledgeable, curious, and opinionated — not algorithmic.

## COPY RULES (from social data)

**Say this:**
- "Ask anything" / "Have a conversation" / "Talks back"
- "Your pace" / "No group" / "Linger as long as you like"
- "Knowledgeable guide" / "Local knowledge" / "Real stories"
- "Hidden gems" / "The story behind the story"

**Never say this:**
- "AI-powered" — users have been burned by AI-slop tours. Position as a guide, not a model.
- "Seamless" — every broken competitor promises seamless. The word is poisoned.
- "Unlock" / "Effortless" / "Powerful" — generic SaaS. Banned.
- "Immersive" — overused to the point of meaninglessness in this category
- Anything that sounds like a Wikipedia summary being read aloud

**Tone standard — the good friend:**
Write like someone who's genuinely excited about this place, not someone doing their fourteenth tour of the day. The copy should feel like a grab of the arm, not a handshake. Opinions are welcome. Enthusiasm is required.
